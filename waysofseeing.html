John Berger: Ways of Seeing

In the first episode of Ways of Seeing, John Berger makes the statement that artwork is 'still and silent.' It is each persons specific perception of the artwork that brings it to life and gives it meaning. For example, looking at a painting with undisturbed silence would be a different than observing it will heavy metal playing in the background. Same goes for if there is slow classical music playing in the background. Basically, Berger points out the atmosphere a piece of art is being observed can completely alter the meaning the person finds in it.

Other factors that may effect how a person is perceiving artwork is how old the person is, the size of the art, the colors portrayed, the tools used, and style of the artwork on display. There are many components that factor into how each person perceives art and this ties into how each person perceives themselves.

I agree with Berger that each person is unique to how they percieve artwork and everyone can find different meanings in the same work of art. I don't think that tis concept ends with artwork. Everybody is unique to themselves, so it is a given that each person percieves the world around them differently.

This observation that Berger explains seems to be very obvious when you spend time and give it thought. But, I honestly hadn't given it much thought prior to watching the videos. Most people probably don't give much thought to this because we think about the things we see as objects and we lose the meaning. What I mean is if a group of people all looked at a picture of a horse, everyone would understand that it was a horse, but everyone would also resonate with the picture differently given there past experiences with horses.

Although I only touched on one specific point Berger made in the first episode, I was able to really dive into the points he made. The ideas he has about how humans percieve artwork are very interesting and can be quite thought provoking.
